About SnapFlash

Built for faster vocabulary learning

A quick look at the problem SnapFlash solves, the learners it is made for, and the value it brings to everyday study.

⚠️

Problem statement

Flashcards are effective for vocabulary retention, but manual card creation is slow and repetitive. The setup effort interrupts reading and reduces learner motivation before studying begins.

🎯

Target users

SnapFlash is designed for Vietnamese students, self-learners, and young professionals who want a faster way to collect vocabulary from textbooks and printed materials.

✨

Value proposition

By combining text capture, word review, deck organization, and lightweight study sessions, SnapFlash helps users spend less time typing and more time learning.

Why it feels this way

Designed to stay simple and focused

The interface is shaped around a few simple decisions that make the product easier to understand and use.

Reduce input friction

The primary entry point prioritizes scanning so users can capture vocabulary directly from printed materials instead of typing everything manually.

Design for fast repetition

Study screens are kept lightweight and focused to support quick review sessions without distraction.

Keep users in control

OCR results are never saved automatically. Users review, select, and edit words before they are added to a deck, which improves trust and accuracy.

Use a structured visual system

A modular design system with clear hierarchy, spacing, and reusable components helps maintain consistency across all screens and deliverables.
